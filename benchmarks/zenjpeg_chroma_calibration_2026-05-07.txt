  photo_or_illustration          → (4:2:2, 0.5)  saves 10-13%
  photo_wide_gamut               → (4:2:2, 0.55) saves  8-11%

Notes:
  - 4:2:2 + low chroma is the dominant pattern across non-natural classes.
    Raising subsampling from 4:2:0 to 4:2:2 keeps full vertical chroma but
    chroma_distance_scale absorbs the bytes back via aggressive chroma RD.
  - photo_natural_or_detailed prefers 4:2:0 + slightly-reduced chroma. Photos
    have smooth chroma gradients where 4:2:0 already does the right thing.
  - illustration_or_logo at 4:2:2/q=85: -15.8% bytes — largest single-knob
    win in the dataset. Sharp-edge content with crushed chroma is free.

Per-image picker would refine further (continuous chroma instead of bin),
but the static-default table above captures the bulk of the available bytes
savings — directly deployable as content-classifier-gated encoder defaults.
